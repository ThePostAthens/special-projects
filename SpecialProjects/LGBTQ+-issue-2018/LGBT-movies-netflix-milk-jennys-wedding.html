7 must-see LGBT movies that will make you feel every emotion

Riley Runnells / For The Post

Admit it, we’ve all had times when we’ve wanted to watch a movie on Netflix but can’t decide which one. Next time you’re looking for a great movie to satisfy your movie night, check out one of these LGBT-centered flicks:

Blue Is the Warmest Color (2013)

Blue Is the Warmest Color is a drama centered on a 15-year-old girl named Adele (Adèle Exarchopoulos) who meets this woman with short blue hair named Emma (Léa Seydoux) that makes her question her sexuality and everything she knows. Adele breaks up with her boyfriend and is sent into emotional turmoil when her friends at school ostracise her. However, she keeps spending time with Emma and their relationship grows. This movie is a beautiful example of a woman coming to terms with her sexuality and understanding who she is and what she wants, even if some people get hurt along the way. This French coming-of-age film is erotic, romantic and definitely one you can’t miss.

Alex Strangelove (2018)

Alex Strangelove is a comedy-drama that lets us in on the life of a kid named Alex Truelove (Daniel Doheny) and his girlfriend Claire (Madeline Weinstein) and they really want to have sex. They have been planning it for a while, and they finally book a hotel room to seal the deal. However when Alex meets a boy named Elliot (Antonio Marziale), he begins to have strong feelings for him that are severely confusing. This film will have you laughing, cringing and smiling ear to ear as you watch this teen navigate his sexual identity.

G.B.F. (2013)

G.B.F. or Gay Best Friend is a teenage comedy about two closeted best friends Tanner (Michael J. Willett) and Brent (Paul Iacono). Brent decides he’s going to out himself at prom because the three most popular girls in school are all searching for a G.B.F. in hopes of securing prom queen. However, with the help of the gay-straight alliance club, Tanner is accidentally outed to the whole school and the attention is geared to him from the popular girls instead of Brent. This sparks a competition between the two best friends as Tanner gains the popularity instead of Brent. This classic high school popularity plot showcases the importance of understanding other people’s sexualities and being proud of who you are no matter what.

3 Generations (2015)

3 Generations is a drama that follows Ramona (Elle Fanning) who was assigned a girl at birth but identifies as a boy named Ray. Ray lives with his single mother, Maggie (Naomi Watts), and his lesbian grandma, Dolly (Susan Sarandon). All of the characters struggle to cope with their varying gender roles. Ray wants to start the testosterone shots and begin the actual process of changing his gender but to do so requires the approval of both parents. The movie revolves around Maggie searching for Ray’s father, who has been absent Ray’s whole life. With some unexpected twists, this movie is a great example of the difficulties with going through a transitional period as a transgender person.

Milk (2008)

Milk is set in 1972 when Harvey Milk (Sean Penn) and his partner Scott Smith (James Franco) move to San Francisco. Milk opens a camera shop and helps transform the area into a really supportive and accepting place for gay people. Then in 1977, Milk becomes the nation’s first openly gay man elected to a notable public office when he is elected to the Board of Supervisors. After a year of serving on the board, one of the other members Dan White (Josh Brolin) kills Milk in cold blood. This biographical-drama and documentary-style film doubles with actual footage and reenactment footage.

King Cobra (2016)

King Cobra is a film that documents the career of Brent Corrigan (Garrett Clayton), a gay porn star, murdered of the 2007 gay pornography producer, Bryan Kocis, who is renamed Stephen Kocis (Christian Slater) in the film. Also starring James Franco and Keegan Allen, this movie has a rockstar cast with so many twists and turns it’ll keep you interested until the last second.

Jenny’s Wedding (2015)

Jenny’s Wedding is a drama that centers around Jenny Farrell (Katherine Heigl), who has always been openly gay, except when it comes to her family. When Jenny becomes tired of her family making fun of her for being lonely, she finally tells them about her partner, Kitty (Alexis Bledel), and how they want to get married. The rest of the movie is Jenny’s family trying to understand that she is gay and Jenny trying to rise above her family’s bigotry.
