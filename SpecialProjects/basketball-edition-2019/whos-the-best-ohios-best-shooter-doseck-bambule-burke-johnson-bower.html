Who’s the best?

2/21/2019

who's ohio's best shooter

JK Kirven / For The Post

When it comes to making 3s, few teams in the Mid-American Conference are better than Ohio. The Bobcats are first in the conference in 3s made (242) and second in percentage (36.8 percent). The reason why the Bobcats are so effective from beyond the arc is because they’re constantly putting shooters on the floor. Four Bobcats are in the top 10 in 3-point percentage and that’s helped produce the highest scoring offensive in the MAC.

Ohio knows how well its shooters compete against other teams, but what if they faced each other? Who would win? The Post takes a look at who might win if the top five shooters on Ohio’s roster faced each other in a 3-point shooting competition.

Meet the favorites.

Burke easily has the most impressive performance of the season so far. In the Bobcats’ win over Ball State on Feb. 2, Burke broke an Ohio and MAC record for 3s in a game (11). She clinched the record with 45 seconds remaining in the third and ended the day an extremely efficient 11-of-15.

Burke is one of the more versatile scorers in the shootoff, meaning her shooting isn’t as refined as some of her competitors. She’ll have a tough time being the best shooter for the entire competition but one amazing round could be all she needs to pull off the upset.

Despite Bambule only averaging just over 12 minutes per game, fans know they amount of havoc she can cause for a defense in that short amount of time. Bambule is the most confident shooter on the team and that makes her a threat in the shootout. The redshirt freshman, however, is also the most inexperienced. Nobody has the ability to dominate the competition and experience is always helpful late in the competition.

The Dark horses

Erica “EJ” Johnson


Accuracy: ★★                  3-point makes-attempts : 36-89
Range : ★★★                  3-point makes-attempts per game: 1.6-4.0
Mechanics: ★★★           3-point percentage: 40.4

Clutchness:★★

Doseck, Bambule and Burke aren’t the only ones on the team that can shoot. Redshirt freshman point guard Johnson also has shown the ability to score from deep. She has a higher percentage than Bambule and more experience, but slightly too much lean on her shot makes her tough to bet on.

Olivia “Shot blocker” Bower


Accuracy: ★★                3-point makes-attempts: 13-41
Range : ★★★                3-point makes-attempts per game: 0.6-1.8
Mechanics: ★★             3-point percentage: 31.7
Experience: ★★★★★
Clutchness: ★★★
Hot streak: ★★

Olivia Bower is the biggest underdog in the competition. She’s the only competitor not in the top 10 rankings for 3-point percentage in the MAC. But don’t read too much into the numbers. She’s got the experience of Doseck and compares well to Burke in most of the categories. In a 3-point shootout, anyone can find momentum and it’s possible that Bower could pull it off.

While this is competition is completely hypothetical, it proves that Ohio is spoiled with fantastic shooting and even though the team is highly competitive, each was high on the others chances.

Burke said that she thinks it would come down to Doseck and Bambule, while Johnson said she’d take Bambule, before learning that she could pick herself. Bambule just loves the fact that the Bobcats can shoot the ball well and spread out the floor.

So, with so much humility, who did Ohio’s coach Bob Boldon choose as his best sharp shooter?

He choose a player outside of the competition: Katie Barker, who’s been out all season rehabbing after leg surgery.
