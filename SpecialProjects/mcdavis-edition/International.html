KELLY BONDRA

08.31.16

College living presents challenges for vegan students

Alexis Eichelberger / For The Post

Ohio University President Roderick McDavis may be leaving OU on Friday, but his legacy will be felt in the campus international community for years to come.

When McDavis joined the university in the summer of 2004, the number of foreign students enrolled at OU during fall 2003 was 1,053.

The latest statistics put out by the university stated that international enrollment for Spring Semester 2016 was 1,648.

In the past several years, OU has received over $2 million in international donations.

While revenue and donations are a factor in entering into partnerships with universities around the world, “the benefits for our students for pursuing research, education and experiential learning are the driving force behind these partnerships,” Lorna Jean Edmonds, vice provost for global affairs, said in an e-mail.

Not all contributions to OU from foreign universities arrive as checks. Chubu University in Japan has a long history of partnering with OU, including donating 175 cherry trees in 1979 and investing in the Yamada International House in 2003.

Chinese students make up the second largest population of students following the United States, with 612 enrolling for the spring semester of 2016 — nearly 500 more than any other nation of origin.

McDavis discussed the importance of international partnerships, particularly Asian institutions, with The Post in 2015.

“Our future relevancy and distinction in the higher education community depends upon our ability to sustain international linkages such as these,” McDavis said in an e-mail.

The international community on campus has mixed opinions on McDavis’ relationship with the international population in Athens, International Student Union President Hashim Pashtun said.

“I know many international students who are happy with his performance, and there are some people who are concerned,” Pashtun said.

In his experience as ISU President Pashtun said McDavis has been very involved with the activities of ISU, including holding monthly meetings with Pashtun.
