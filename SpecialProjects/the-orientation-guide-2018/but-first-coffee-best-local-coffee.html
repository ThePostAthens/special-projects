But First, Coffee

05.31.18

Here’s where you can find the best coffee deals in Athens

Erin Gardner / For The Post

Coffee on campus is a necessity, but let’s face it: coffee can do some damage on the wallet, especially on a college budget. An average specialty drink can cost anywhere from $3 to $5 on a good day. Most shops offer loyalty cards or weekly deals to help the avid caffeine consumer stay afloat. The Post is here to help with the inside scoop on which coffee shops are handing out deals:

Donkey Coffee and Espresso, 17 W. Washington St.

Alexandria Skowronski | FOR THE POST

Chris Innis poses for a portrait in his favorite chair at Donkey Coffee. Innis comes into Donkey Coffee every morning to read the paper and get the daily roast, black — except on Saturdays, when he gets a latte.

Donkey has two floors and a balcony for students to study or chat with friends. Its free Wi-Fi and locally sourced coffee makes Donkey a personal favorite. Every Tuesday at 9 p.m., Donkey has Designated Space, which is an open stage dedicated to poetry, prose and spoken word. Every Thursday at 8 p.m., there is an Open Stage hosted by Allison DeWitt. Donkey’s loyalty card is a great deal where each specialty drink adds a point that amounts to a free drink, coffee beans and, ultimately, a Donkey sweatshirt. Additionally, 10 percent is taken off the price when customers bring in reusable mug.

Court Street Coffee, 67 S. Court St.

Court Street Coffee is easy to access and has plenty of seating. With its house drink, the Court Street Mocha and local bagels, the quaint and cozy shop is the perfect stop for a lazy day. During Bobcat Student Orientation, Court Street is giving new students a free size upgrade when students show their orientation bag.

Whit’s Frozen Custard, 49 S. Court St.

Whit’s is a fan favorite that is famous for its frozen custard, but the location also features lattes, red eyes and chai. Every day, $1 refills are offered when a reusable mug is brought in.

Brenen’s Coffee Cafe, 38 S. Court St.

Brenen’s is a cafe that sells coffee, tea, bagels, muffins and in-house sandwiches. Right on Court Street, Brenen’s is the place to go for Saturday brunch with the girls. During orientation, the specialty drink is a honey vanilla latte served hot or cold. Additionally, students with their badge on get a large for the price of a small.

Town’s End Coffee, 9133 Rock Riffle Road

Located just outside campus, Town’s End Coffee is a newly founded shop. With delicious coffee, fruit and ethically sourced chocolate, Town’s End is the perfect spot to get away from the busyness of the weekend.

Fluff Bakery, 8 N. Court St.

Fluff Bakery features a wide variety of specialty drinks and a decorated drink board that is updated weekly. Carrying bagels, cupcakes and cinnamon rolls, Fluff is a great option for a lazy breakfast. Fluff has $1 off a large espresso and specialty drinks for those who have their orientation badge, but it’s limited to one drink per person.
