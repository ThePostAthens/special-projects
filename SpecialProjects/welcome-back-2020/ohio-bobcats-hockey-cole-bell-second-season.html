New Season, New Approach

August 27, 2020

Hockey: Cole Bell preparing for growing pains in second season

By Jack Gleckler | Sports Editor

  • Share on Facebook
  • Tweet
  • Send email

C ole Bell wasn’t expecting his first season as head coach at Ohio to be cut short. The coronavirus changed those plans and have made the 2020-2021 season’s future murky at best. Bell must now prepare for another season which may end sooner than anyone in the American Collegiate Hockey Association wants it to.

Despite the uncertainty of the upcoming season, Bell hasn’t shied away from his duties amid the pandemic. He’s met with his coaching staff at least twice a week since leaving Athens and is excited about his return to Bird Arena. The most important goal for him is to make a leap forward from last season.

“I’m not doing my job as a coach if I’m not trying to learn and get better.” Bell said. “We’ve made adjustments to our structure. We’re trying our best to get all the pieces together for the start of the year.”

The adjustments to structure made by Bell include a new member of the coaching staff. Leo Mauron, the new graduate assistant coach for the Bobcats, has been well-received by Bell thanks to his willingness to jump directly into his new role.

Mauron is currently the only new coach that has been named. Former graduate assistant coach Travis Hyde graduated and former analytics coach Erich Junge is unlikely to return. Gehrig Sarosy, the current analytics coach for Ohio, is the only staff member that is confirmed to return from last season.

A new set of coaches comes at the same time the Bobcats begin a dramatic change in their lineup. Longtime starter Jimmy Thomas’ departure has spawned Ohio’s biggest question —who will be the new starting goalie?

Three new goalies have been recruited to Ohio: Matt Server, Max Karlenzig and Parker Ray. The race for starter is further contentious when returning goalies Jackson Chilberg and Mason Koster are thrown in.

The goalie question is the only major problem confronting the Bobcats this summer. However, summer rest always comes with the regression that early practices can get rid of. Bell is confident in the team to have a rapid return to form. The Bobcats will be gunning for a strong start after losing all hope for a national tournament back in March.

“I think last season will get us hot out of the gate,” Bell said. “That’s not unique to us, but we’re all excited to get back on the ice and right now it’s just a waiting game.”

When the Bobcats returns to Athens for practice, Bell wants to tinker with a new strategy—a more aggressive defense.

The Bobcats’ defense has always been assertive. Bell wants to better utilize that on the ice. He referred to the defensemen as good “north-south” skaters that love to rush the puck. Freshman defenseman Sam Turner is the prime example of Ohio’s aggressive defense. Turner earned 17 points last season from six goals and 11 assists, the second-highest among defenders for Ohio.

Reliable young talent like Turner and the experience of veterans like Shawn Baird will make sure Ohio’s defense excels with more allotted freedom on the ice.

“We trust our defense to make good reads on the ice,” Bell said. “We have an intelligent core and we want to get them involved as much as possible.”

The adjustments to Ohio’s approach this season are bound to have growing pains. New additions to the coaching staff, lineup and strategy will hopefully shift the Bobcats toward a stronger program.

Bell just hopes the culture of success will continue next season.

“I think Ohio’s identity on the ice and in the locker room is allowed to change from year to year,” Bell said. “Ultimately we’ve always had this tradition of being successful. It’s not a right to play at Ohio, it’s a privilege. That’s something we need to honor everyday.”
