Volleyball: A preview of the 2020 season

By Scott Thomas | For The Post

  • Share on Facebook
  • Tweet
  • Send email

T he Ohio volleyball team will start the 2020 season with more questions than answers after a hectic offseason.

If the season does happen, it won’t start until at least Sept. 3. As for right now the nonconference schedule is uncertain. The Bobcats don’t have any matches scheduled until their Mid-American Conference opener against Akron on Sept. 25.

Ohio is also yet to find out the impact of the year-long probation due to its NCAA recruiting violations that resulted in its last three seasons being vacated.

Perhaps the largest question that remains pertaining to on-court play is the replacement of outside hitter Lizzie Stephens, who was one of the strongest players in program history.

The most likely candidate to fill that position is junior Lauren Park, who received the bulk of the playing time when Stephens went down with an injury last season. Park played in 21 matches last season and had 147 kills.

She will be challenged by incoming transfer student Mariana Rodrigues from Indian Hills Community College. The junior is a two-time NJCAA All-American who recorded 1,090 kills in just two seasons.

Otherwise, the Bobcats will have a veteran team with four returning starters. The two anchors of the starting rotation that will be returning are MAC Setter of the Year Vera Giacomazzi and MAC Player of the Year and Defensive Player of the Year Tia Jimerson.

The setter-middle duo has dominated the MAC over the last two years. Giacomazzi finished last season with the most assists in the conference and 13th most in the nation. Jimerson led the MAC with a .369 hitting percentage and had 1.33 blocks per set, also the most in the conference.

Lining up beside Jimerson, senior Maggie Nedoma will return for her second season with the Bobcats after transferring from Southern Illinois University. The lefty was fifth in the conference with 3.29 kills per set last season.

The Bobcats will also return sophomore Olivia Margolies, who hit extremely well in several matches with double-digit kills, and redshirt junior Dahlias Bouyer, who saw plenty of action in the beginning of the season while Jimerson was absent.

In the back row, Macy Reihing will return as the libero. Reihing averaged 4.56 digs per set last season.

The last remaining starting spot in the back row is up for grabs at the moment. Last season, Ciara Livingway and Emma Tupa were the ones who saw the most playing time off the bench. Tupa also received some time at libero.

Ohio finished in good standings last season with an 18-13 overall record and 9-7 in the MAC, good enough for third in the MAC East. The teams to beat in the division will be Miami and Bowling Green. The Bobcats went 1-3 against the two top teams with two losses to Bowling Green, one of which was a sweep.

The 2020 season will undoubtedly be different than last year. Spectators are unlikely, and the season being played at all is still uncertain. The uncertainty and the difficult offseason will create an unprecedented challenge for head coach Deane Webb and the coaching staff.
