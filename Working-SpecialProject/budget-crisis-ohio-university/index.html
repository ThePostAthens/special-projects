Introduction

Ohio University’s budget affects faculty, staff and administration members as well as students. There are predictions that 40 to 50 instructional staff will lose their jobs in Arts and Sciences alone, Loren Lybarger, president of the OU chapter of the American Association for University Professors, said.

The AAUP and OU Fun Facts have scheduled round tables, rallies and protests to discuss and challenge the idea of budget cuts that would cause faculty members to lose their jobs.

The quality of the education at OU is partially what attracts students to the university, so firing or laying off faculty members who contribute to students’ education could be detrimental.

The university’s budget shows that the university is only budgeted to earn $1.6 million while it made $65.6 million as recently as 2017.

The Post will be following the “budget crisis” as it develops because it affects many people within the university, including students and faculty and staff members. This page serves to inform students and university employees about the university’s budget. It will be continually updated to reflect the most recent information.
