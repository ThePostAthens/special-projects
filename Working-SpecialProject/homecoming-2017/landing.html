Something special might be brewing in Peden Stadium.

 

In August, there was uncertainty surrounding the entire team. A.J. Ouellette needed to show he had recovered from a Lisfranc tear and the defense needed to prove that it could replace a front seven that lost three defenders to the NFL.

 

After five games, there’s a pretty clear answer.

 

Ohio heads to this weekend at 4-1 and 1-0 in the Mid-American Conference. The Bobcats aren’t there yet. But they’re close.

 

A quick look at the schedule shows it’s hard not to look ahead. Bowling Green is winless and Kent State’s quarterback tore his ACL. They’re upcoming on the schedule.

 

But before that, Ohio will have to get through Central Michigan on Saturday.

 

The following pages and stories detail the Bobcats run through the first five games and what could be for the following weeks. There’s profiles of standout players, stories about their off the field fun and whatever else you could want.
