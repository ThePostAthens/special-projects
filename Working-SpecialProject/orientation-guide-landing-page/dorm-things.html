Students discuss residence hall necessities

08.31.16

Students discuss what they need and don’t need to bring to campus

Anastasia Nicholas / For The Post

Many incoming Ohio University freshmen move away from home for the first time when they get to college and might be at a complete loss of what they need to pack.

OU’s move-in guide lists about 20 items to bring to campus — including blankets and sheets, clothes hangers and comfortable walking shoes — but some students have their own unique suggestions.

“A little pet fish is always fun,” Rachel Starrett, an undecided rising sophomore, said.

Erika Matie said she wrongly assumed she would eat all her meals in the dining hall. She said she could’ve used a bowl for when she was eating in her room.

“I would’ve loved to have a bowl because I could’ve used it for ramen, or soup in general,” Matie, a rising sophomore studying pre-social work, said. “I have canned food, and you can’t put a can in the microwave.”

The OU move-in guide also has nearly 20 “nice but not necessary” items including a television, a fan and a bike.

Kristin Tomoff said she brought a television but ended up not using it.

“I don’t need one because the dorm lobby has one,” Tomoff, an undecided rising sophomore, said. “All my friends have TVs. I don’t really use mine.”

On the other hand, Spencer Wilson said he likes having a TV.

“Although some people might say it takes away from you doing other things, it's nice to have to watch Netflix or sports or something else,” Wilson, a rising senior studying sport management, said. “It's a nice study break when necessary.”

Another item the university lists as “nice but not necessary” is a fan.

“It's good for white noise to help you fall asleep, and it helps keep a good temperature in the room,” Wilson said. “I had to buy one when I came to campus, instead of bringing the one from my room at home.”

Wyatt Brady said he suggests bringing curtains to campus.

“I live right next to streetlights and lamps,” Brady, a rising sophomore studying psychology and biological sciences, said. “At night, my dorm gets really bright, and it’s hard to sleep.”
