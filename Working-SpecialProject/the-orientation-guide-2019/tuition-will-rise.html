Tuition Will Rise

05.30.19

A look at the finances at OU, including increased tuition

Ian McKenzie / Asst. News Editor

The Ohio University Board of Trustees approved a 3.5 percent tuition increase in their January meeting.

If the State of Ohio approves a 2 percent state tuition cap, Ohio residents’ annual tuition will increase from about $12,192 to about $12,618, according to a previous Post report.

The housing payment would increase from about $6,822 to about $7,060 for Ohio residents. Dining costs would increase from about $5,790 to about $5,905.

The increase in tuition is partly due to the amount of college-bound students in the Midwest decreasing, Chaden Djalali, executive vice president and provost, said in a previous Post report.

This is the first time tuition has increased in four years.

Tuition is based on the amount of credit hours students are taking. If a student is full time, which is a student taking more than 12 credit hours, the cost is capped until 20 credit hours.

The normal amount of credit hours is 15 for one semester.

For Ohio residents, any more than 20 credit hours is a $247 fee per credit hour and for out-of-state students, the fee is $478 per credit hour.

The full cost for tuition for full-time Ohio residents below 20 credit hours is $5,372 while the out-of-state total is $10,010.
